Procedural modeling of 3D fractal geometry (Working title)

Chethna Kabeerdoss


Description of Problem

Fractals, they are not just pretty computer generated images that are popularly used as background wallpapers. Most physical systems of nature and many human artifacts are not regular geometric shapes of the standard geometry derived from Euclid. Fractal geometry offers almost unlimited ways of describing, measuring and predicting these natural phenomena. These ‘Patterns of chaos’ can be found in many places – membrane of a cell, leaves in trees, crystals formed under the earth, to the rings of Saturn. They are self-similar patterns. We are so used to seeing them as 2D images, what if these fractals were to be procedurally model as a 3D geometry?


Importance of Problem

Finding a way to transform the 2D equations that govern a fractal structure to an equation that can represent it in 3 dimensions. This project deals with procedurally modeling 3D geometry, which has its roots from a 2D fractal representation. The 3D structure will also be governed by some the fractal rules it’s predecessor was.

Originality

The approach I am considering is replicating an artist’s 2D work (paintings). Observe the style and choose a part that has self-similar pattern (like the foliage, rivulets etc) in a 3D geometric form. I would define these rules that would do which would help shape the 2D to 3D representation of the artists work.


List of Goals

• Pick an artist’s work that interests me, which has self-similar patterns.

• Narrow down the kind of 3D fractal geometry I would like to create from the 2D representation.

• Figure out the rules (L systems, maybe using turtle graphics) that would translate this 2D representation into a 3D geometry.

• Find out how best to render this geometry.
